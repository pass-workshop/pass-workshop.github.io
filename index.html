Personal Agent Systems Foundations, Infrastructure, and Personalization

NeurIPS 2026 Workshop

Personal agent systems are AI systems that understand individual users, maintain long-term context, coordinate tools and services, and adapt their behavior to personal goals, preferences, and constraints. As foundation models become more capable agents, the central challenge is shifting from isolated task solvers to persistent, trustworthy, and useful assistants that can operate across devices, applications, modalities, and real-world routines.

The PAS workshop brings together researchers working on the foundations, infrastructure, benchmarks, and personalization methods required for personal agents. We emphasize reliable reasoning, human-agent interaction, privacy-preserving memory, scalable deployment, and responsible adaptation over time.

Topics of Interest Personal Agent Systems
1
Foundations of Personal Agent Systems User modeling, intent understanding, preference learning, long-term memory, and reasoning or planning across language, vision, web, mobile, and embodied contexts.
2
Infrastructure for Personal Agents Memory stores, retrieval, tool use, workflow orchestration, agent operating environments, provenance, rollback, interoperability, and privacy controls.
3
Personalization and Human-Agent Interaction Adaptive interfaces, controllable proactive assistance, continual personalization, and user-centered applications in web, GUI, mobile, robotics, accessibility, education, healthcare, and creative workflows.
4
Benchmarking, Evaluation, and Datasets Benchmarks, datasets, simulators, toolkits, and metrics for long-horizon, user-centered, tool-using agents, including task success, personalization quality, reliability, privacy, and trust.
5
Ethics, Interpretability, and Reliability Safe action execution, consent, oversight, rollback, explainability, auditability, accountability, user ownership, fairness, bias mitigation, and misuse prevention.
Call for Papers Personal Agent Systems

Submission Tracks

We invite submissions on the foundations, infrastructure, personalization, evaluation, and responsible deployment of personal agent systems. We welcome original research, position papers, benchmark and dataset papers, system demonstrations, and work-in-progress that can help define the next generation of trustworthy personal AI agents.


Submission Guidelines

The workshop will use a double-blind review process. Submissions should be anonymized and will receive 3-4 reviews, including ethical review where appropriate. Detailed formatting instructions and the OpenReview submission link will be posted after workshop acceptance.

Accepted papers, posters, talk titles, and the final program will be made available online through the workshop website to support broad access to workshop materials.
